Mushroom Butter Masala

The dish typically features button mushrooms or a combination of mushroom varieties, cooked with onions, garlic, and ginger, and then simmered in a creamy sauce made with butter, milk or cream, and cashews or almonds. The cashews or almonds add a creamy texture and balance the tanginess of the tomatoes, without the need for heavy cream. This versatile sauce can be served with a variety of accompaniments, including Basmati rice, Jeera rice, Ghee rice, roti, chapati, or plain parathas.

Price: ₹ 350

Aloo Tikki

Whip up some vibrant flavours with aloo tikki, a popular North Indian snack! A delightful play of spices and potato,it's as exciting to make as it is to savour. Get ready to tease your taste buds and explore the culinary culture of India.Enjoy Aloo Tikki as a standalone snack, served hot and crispy straight from the pan. Pair it with a tangy tamarind chutney or mint yogurt dip for an extra burst of flavor.Blooming the spices in a tablespoon of oil does make their flavors more pronounced and distributes them more evenly.

Price: ₹ 50

Paneer kaju bhurji

Paneer Kaju Bhurji is a popular North Indian dish that combines the creaminess of paneer (Indian cottage cheese) with the richness of cashews (kaju). This flavorful and aromatic dish is made by cooking a spicy onion-tomato masala with crumbled paneer, cashews, and a blend of ground spices.

Price: ₹ 500

Samosa

The samosa is prepared with an all-purpose flour (locally known as maida) and stuffed with a filling, often a mixture of diced and cooked or mashed boiled potato, onions, green peas, lentils, ginger, spices and green chili.A samosa can be vegetarian or non-vegetarian, depending on the filling. The entire pastry is deep-fried in vegetable oil or (rarely) ghee to a golden brown.

Price: ₹ 60

Paav Bhaji

Pav Bhaji is a popular Indian street food originating from Mumbai (formerly Bombay). It’s a hearty, flavorful dish consisting of a rich, buttery vegetable curry served with soft, fluffy bread rolls called “pav.” The curry, known as “bhaji,” is made with a medley of vegetables, spices, and herbs, cooked in a mixture of oil and butter.The dish is characterized by a thick, spicy vegetable curry (bhaji) served with a soft, buttered bread roll (pav).

Price: ₹ 150

Khasta Kachori

Khasta Kachori is a type of crispy, flaky, and hollow Indian snack pastry originating from Rajasthan. It is a deep-fried, puffed pastry filled with a spicy moong dal mixture. The exterior is characterized by its khasta (crispy, crackly, flaky, brittle) texture, while the interior remains hollow. The filling typically consists of a mixture of baked moong dal, besan (gram flour), coriander, red chili powder, and salt.

Price: ₹ 100
